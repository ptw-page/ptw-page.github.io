Ancient Mirror

A copper mirror is a common everyday instrument found in ancient China. Since ancient times, copper mirrors have not only played an important role in people's daily lives, but also contain a rich culture, intuitively embodying the copper casting crafts, aesthetic tastes, and folk beliefs of that era, and have been a crossroads of cultural exchanges between China and foreign countries. It has served as a model and is an important material medium for excellent traditional culture in China.

Ancient Sword

The earliest archaeological relics were found in the Shang Dynasty in the Bronze Age of China, and it is called Jikbaedo (直背刀). As the name suggests, it is straight or curved, and is single-edged. It was originally made of bronze, but in the latter part of the Warring States period, iron or steel weapons began to appear. Originally, it was used less often than a straight double-edged sword, but it began to be widely used mainly by cavalry during the Han Dynasty.
